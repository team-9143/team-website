Dustbin

2023 Robot

CAD and Code

Structural

● 26” x 32” frame perimeter for narrow footprint on charge station ● KOP chassis frame ● Two-piece C bumpers with reversible covers ● Acrylic belly pan with ventilation slots ● Acrylic top cover to protect electronics ● Padded resting supports for intake arm ● Reinforced and higher support brackets for intake arm ● 6” high grip wheels (middle) and smooth grip wheels (corners) ● In-match Robot weight: ~80.0 lbs ● Bumper weight: ~13.5 lbs

Autonomous

● Modular selector to customize auton scoring ● Auton start: shoot, spit, shoot down, spit down ● Auton body: long backwards, short backwards, pickup cone, center over backward, center backward ● Auton end: turn around, none

Teleop

● Speed: ~16.6 ft/s ● Agile robot movement with quick turning ● Pick up cubes and cones from the ground ● Cone scoring in hybrid nodes ● Cube scoring in all nodes (shooting to mid or high nodes)

Mechanical

● West Coast drive train ● 4 NEO V1.1 brushless motors on drive train ● Modified Everybot intake (narrowed to cube width) ● 2 NEO V1.1 brushless tilt motors on intake with 90° gearboxes (35:1 ratio each) (new after SFR) ● 1 NEO V1 brushless spin motor on intake (3:1 ratio)

End Game

● Dock and engage the charge station ● Auto-balancing program

Electrical

● Standard power, CAN bus, and Ethernet wiring with cable clips for connection security ● REV power distribution hub ● CTRE Voltage regulator module ● NI roboRIO 2.0 (1.0 as backup) ● REV radio power module ● AndyMark Radio ● CTRE Pigeon 2.0 ● 7 Spark MAX motor controllers

Strategy

● Act as a shuttle robot to bring cubes (preferred) and cones back to the Community ● Score on mid or high nodes with cubes to increase point value ● Score on hybrid nodes with cubes (preferred) or cones to complete links and to support alliance partners ● Balance on charge station ● Tilt charge station for alliance partners

Controls

● JAVA programming ● Dashboard running off of Shuffleboard interface
